WHY SUPPORT THIS EVENT?

NgConf Colombia 2020 is a non-profit event and 100% of our income will be destined to provide a unique experience to all participants: attendees, speakers and sponsoring entities. The short and medium-term benefits for the development of our region and country as the epicenter of the fourth industrial revolution will result in more employment and a more motivated audience to learn new technologies.

The sponsorship support we get will make it possible for us to achieve our objectives and to support those who participate in it. Gathering so many benefits in a single event will keep our region updated and help to maintain the growth trend and finally will allow foreign investment to keep our eyes on our city recognizing it as a city that bets on technology and offers quality solutions.

COMMUNITY IN BOGOTÁ

In 2018, this community was created under the name of ng-Colombia Bogotá which, through periodic meetings of technical talks, seeks to expose the benefits of using Angular as the main Frontend development framework. As of today, there have been 5 meetups with more than 250 attendees impacted.

COMMUNITY IN MEDELLIN

The holding of this conference in our country, and especially in this region, is not an isolated event. It comes as a result of the constant work that the Angular Medellín community has been doing for the past 3 years in hopes of improving the skills of the Frontend developers. Thanks to monthly meetings with keynote talks and practical workshops, around 85 attendees were impacted which is an average that exceeds more than 3 times that achieved in the same events highlighted in Europe and/or the United States.

We would like your company to be part of this magnificent event as one of our sponsors. Thanks to your help, our country will continue to be a pioneer in providing this experience in Latin America in addition to continuing to bring the best benefits to our region.

WOMEN LEARNING ANGULAR

In Medellín, our community often helds workshops aimed at women, seeking to reduce the gender gap in the software development environment. As of today, we already had more than 6 workshops with an attendance rate that surpasses 100 people per event.
