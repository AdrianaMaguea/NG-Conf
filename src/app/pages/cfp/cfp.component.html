To bear in mind

Brief recommendations:

Participating is very easy, only take into account the following guidelines when proposing your talk:

  • Choose a topic that is within the recommended ones.
  • Make sure that the selected topic is valid and updated.
  • Do what you can to make sure the topic has a positive impact on the audience
  • Avoid as much as possible that the subject matter belongs to proprietary software so that the audience can use it freely.
  • If the topic is very extensive, try to choose the most relevant and interesting characteristics.

Selection of proposals

Criteria

In order to have a fair and transparent selection process for speakers, all proposals received will be evaluated in the same way, regardless of the experience that the participant has giving talks at conferences.

For this purpose, we have defined the following selection criteria that will be given relevance during the evaluation process that will be in charge of a committee external to the organization of the event.

  • Relevance

    The selected topic is related to the Angular ecosystem, Web Technologies and non-technical topics focused on software development.

  • Novelty

    The topic of the talk is new or is current for our job as developers. Includes new features already available or about to be released.

  • Impact

    The intervention will have a positive impact on the varied and diverse audience. It is a theme that improves or takes our skills as developers to the next level.

  • Clearness

    The title and description of the intervention are presented clearly and concisely. The objective to be achieved is easily interpreted.

Evaluation

The following selection process aims to choose the best proposals for talks and workshops for our event.

  • Presentation of proposals: open until May 31, 2020
  • Selection of a committee external to the organization made up of 6 mixed people who have not sent a proposal for a talk or workshop, who have intermediate to advanced knowledge of Angular
  • Committee members will receive all proposals anonymously, which means that they will not know the identity of the proponent.
  • Members will evaluate and score each proposal according to the aforementioned criteria.
  • The organization of the event will receive all the evaluations and will add the score obtained, choosing the talks with the highest scores that fit our mesh.
  • The non-elected people will remain on the waiting list, in case any of the chosen speakers cannot make their intervention. They will also receive tickets to attend our conference.
  • The people chosen as speakers will be notified via email to start the advisory process.
